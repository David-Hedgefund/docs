David is designed to mirror the breadth of a real market-data vendor while remaining fully synthetic.

## Universe size

- The universe contains **16,143 unique public US symbols** drawn from SEC/Nasdaq snapshots.
- The scale target is **27,000 active and delisted US tickers** per scenario, matching a full licensed universe.
- Each scenario in the library addresses up to **16,143 real ticker aliases**.

## Horizons

Scenarios deliberately mix time horizons rather than forcing one length:

| Horizon | Typical span |
| --- | --- |
| Full panel | ~30 years |
| Business-cycle panel | several years |
| Annual event study | ~1 year |
| Focused event window | weeks to months |

A scenario's actual span is given by its `start_date` and `end_date`. Trading days are synthetic calendar days. See [Date semantics](/concepts/date-semantics).

## Scale presets

David defines presets that trade off scenario count, ticker count, and horizon. Fetch them live via [`GET /metadata/scale-presets`](/api-reference/metadata#scale-presets).

| Preset | Scenarios | Tickers | Span |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Library | 720 | real aliases | mixed horizons |
| Research panel | 250 | 1,000 | ~3 years |
| Full scale | 1,000 | 27,000 | ~30 years |

<Info>
  Company facts, prices, fundamentals, filings, news, events, and ownership are generated deterministically from the scenario/ticker seed, giving broad real-symbol coverage across the universe.
</Info>

## Coverage map

David implements a Financial Datasets–style endpoint surface, native endpoints plus derived compatibility views (as-reported statements, segments, screeners, KPI/non-GAAP metrics, index funds, interest rates, market snapshots). The live coverage map is available at [`GET /metadata/financial-datasets-coverage`](/api-reference/metadata#financial-datasets-coverage).
